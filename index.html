Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Here are some tips to enhance your financial cybersecurity:

  • Watch What You Click:

    Avoid clicking on suspicious links. Confirm information directly through official channels to prevent exposure to malware.

  • Use Strong Passwords:

    Don’t share passwords; use unique ones for each account. Consider using a password manager for better protection.

  • Enable Multifactor Authentication (MFA):

    Implement MFA for added account security. Utilize two or more authentication factors for enhanced protection.

  • Maintain Device Security:

    Install updated security software with antivirus features. Keep devices and software updated for optimal security.

  • Use Your Own Device:

    Avoid public computers for financial transactions. Secure your mobile devices and financial apps with strong credentials.

  • Browse Carefully:

    Verify secure website addresses (https). Log out after accessing financial accounts and avoid multitasking.

  • Be Cyber Safe When Using Wi-Fi:

    Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. Secure home Wi-Fi with strong encryption and passwords.

  • Review Financial Correspondence:

    Regularly check account activity and statements. Report any mistakes or unauthorized activity promptly.

  • Use Security Software That Updates Automatically:

    Keep security software, operating systems, and browsers up-to-date. Be cautious of unexpected pop-ups and emails regarding security software.

  • Treat Your Personal Information Like Cash:

    Only share personal information with trusted entities. Be skeptical of requests for personal information and phishing attempts.

  • Check Out Companies to Find out Who You’re Really Dealing With:

    Research companies before making online transactions. Verify legitimacy and avoid deals with insufficient information.

  • Give Personal Information Over Encrypted Websites Only:

    Stick to encrypted sites for online transactions. Look for "https" on every page of the website, not just the sign-in page.

  • Protect Your Passwords:

    Create strong, unique passwords with at least 10 characters. Avoid using the same password across multiple accounts.

  • Back Up Your Files:

    Regularly back up important files to an external device. Ensure access to files in case of a compromised computer.
